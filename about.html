About the Author

Jasper Vale is a name that emerges from the twilight, a guide for readers willing to step beyond the veil of the familiar. With words that bleed from the page and language that floats like mist through half-lit corridors, Vale crafts stories that do not seek to explain the unknown, but to draw readers into its depths.

Behind this name, a shadow flickers—Ryan Coupe, the hidden architect whose own journey through light and darkness bring to light worlds that challenge and unsettle. Little is known of Vale’s personal history—by design. Each novel is a pilgrimage into the unseen, where silence becomes a companion and shadows bear witness.

Three Days Hollow—Vale’s latest work—ventures into the hidden corridors of mortality itself, exploring the terrain between death and resurrection. Here, grief becomes a compass, guiding characters through realms where hope flickers like a candle flame in the darkness.

When the pen is set aside, Vale can often found treading wooded paths at dusk, lost in the depths of ancient portraits, or leaning into the thunderous hush between lightning strikes—forever drawn to the places where eternity’s secrets breathe in the dark.
